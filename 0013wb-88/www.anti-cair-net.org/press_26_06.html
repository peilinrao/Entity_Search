     CAIR:  No Comment on Torture-Murdered American Soldiers

      On 20 June, two soldiers, Pfc. Kristian Menchaca of Houston, TX. and Pfc. Thomas Tucker of Madras, OR. were taken by Muslim terrorists at a military checkpoint in Iraq.

     The soldier’s bodies were later recovered and displayed evidence of brutal torture before death. 

     In the past, the Council on American-Islamic Relations (CAIR); an Islamic terrorist supporting hate group, has run to the nearest microphone to proclaim to the world that Islam does not condone the mutilation of bodies, even the bodies of infidels. 

     This time?  Silence.

     Why?  Would one possible reason be that CAIR realizes that nothing they say will make sense to anyone with any knowledge of what they are and what they represent?  Have Americans finally caught on to the con job that CAIR is running against us?  That Americans don’t particularly care for a “bought and paid for” Islamic terrorist front group working out of our nation’s capital? 

     Or could it be that CAIR has simply run out rhetoric to explain away the brutal torture of American soldiers who are fighting, and dying, to protect America and bring peace to a tormented land?

     In either case, the silence from CAIR is very welcome.   

     There is no place in America for an Islamic hate group.
