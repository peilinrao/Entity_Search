About Our Doors

 

Door form meets function

Exterior doors and entry systems can make a bold and impactful first impression that reflects your tastes and compliments your architectural environment. What's more, these doors fulfill an important functional role by protecting your home from the environment, providing privacy and insuring security. Whether it's an entrance door and a transom, or paired with sidelites, French or ‘patio' doors offer wonderful flexibility and versatility as well.

 

Feature door systems, such as Operating Sidelite Systems, Surround Systems and Sliding Door Systems allow you to tailor the door configurations dependent upon your practical needs and design preferences.

 

Because they're not exposed to the elements, interior doors are available in virtually every species, texture and finish—in a variety of styles—to compliment your exterior doors and color palette as well as the architecture of your interior space.

 

All-Panel and All-Glass Combinations allow you to experiment with various glass and panel combinations to create a more open or more private feeling door. Door tops allow you to create the door shape that suits your unique style and compliments your environment, whether you prefer the simple lines of a square top, or the graceful elegance of an arch. Our custom door top shapes such as flat arch, true arch, and elliptical arch, invite you to play with possibilities and lines of your space. Many of our doors feature a wide spectrum of glass options to create a more refreshing, bright feel.

 

You draw the lines

Choose the panel profile and sticking option that suits your style by determining the lines and dimensions of your door, and giving it a truly customized look. A Mission sticking with a raised panel offers a more elegant Southwest feel; while a classic sticking with a plank panel can exude a more rustic, simple tone; glass, in place of wood panels, or a combination of wood and glass creates a completely different feel that invites the outside in, while still insuring privacy.

 

The wood that works

At Architectural Traditions, we offer a selection of more than nine spectacular wood species from around the world. Each one is individual, with no other one like it on earth, able to capture an equally distinctive look: from the refined Classic elegance of a deep mahogany, or a more rustic Country feel of a knotty Alder. Extreme heat, constant moisture, fluctuations in snow and sun, and ocean breezes will impact your wood choice as well. While virtually all wood species are appropriate for interior applications, we recommend our ‘exposure protected’ application for long-term durability and minimal maintenance of all our exterior doors. Cedar and Mahogany are some lovely wood species that are most appropriate in the most severe climates.

 

Finish with style

At Architectural Traditions, we offer a spectrum of standard and customized finishes to enhance the patina and grain patterns, and bring your door to life. Using traditional Old World methods, our skilled artisans can also create a rubbed oil finish that unleashes the radiant luster of the wood, creating a wonderfully authentic look and protecting it from long-term environmental effects. Unique to Architectural Traditions, any of our finishes can have a "glaze" added to add dimension and highlights to your door. Create a weathered, rugged, antique or refined look with our selection of 14 distress textures.

 

A tasteful touch

A charming speakeasy, a sparkling glass inset or an Old World wood inlay can emphasize the rich character and history of your door. They are the accents that make your statement truly unique.
